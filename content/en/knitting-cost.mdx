# Knitting Cost Calculator — Pricing Your Handmade Products

Determining the right price for handmade items is one of the most challenging tasks for
artisans and boutique creators. Simply adding a small amount on top of material costs
often means your labor isn't fully compensated. Proper pricing is critical for a
sustainable hobby or business.

## What are the Cost Components?

A professional cost calculation should include these four main items:

1.  **Material Cost:** The total cost of the main material (yarn) and secondary items
    (buttons, zippers, labels, decorations).
2.  **Labor (Effort):** The monetary value of your time. If a sweater takes 20 hours
    to knit, you should set an hourly rate for yourself for this time.
3.  **Overheads (Shipping/Packaging):** The cost of shipping the product and packaging
    expenses (boxes, paper, ribbons).
4.  **Profit Margin:** The percentage you add on top of all costs to grow your business
    or earn beyond just your labor.

## How is it Calculated?

Our calculator uses the following basic formulas:

**Total Cost:**
> Material Cost + (Work Hours × Hourly Rate) + Shipping/Packaging

**Recommended Sales Price:**
Suggested price based on your desired profit margin (e.g., 50%):
> Total Cost × (1 + Profit Margin / 100)

**Profit Analysis:**
If you have a sales price in mind, we find how much profit you make from it:
> Profit = Sales Price - Total Cost
> Profit Margin = (Profit / Total Cost) × 100

## Tips for Setting Your Labor Rate

Valuing your own effort can be difficult. Consider the following as starting points:
-   **Minimum Wage Based:** Base it on the hourly equivalent of the minimum wage in
    your area.
-   **Experience Based:** Increase your hourly rate as your mastery level grows.
-   **Market Analysis:** Research the price range for similar quality products in
    the market.

## Disclaimer

This calculator is based on generally accepted cost principles for boutique producers.
Taxes (VAT, withholding, etc.) and platform commissions (like Etsy, Shopier) are not
included in the calculation. Consider these extra expenses when determining the
final price.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How should I determine my hourly labor rate?**
Consider your experience and the complexity of the task. For starters, you can base
it on the hourly equivalent of the minimum wage.

**What expenses should I add as 'accessories'?**
You can add buttons, zippers, labels, packaging materials, and any secondary materials
used for decoration in this section.

**What should the profit margin be?**
Generally, a profit margin between 30% and 100% is applied to boutique handmade
products. Consider market conditions and your effort.

**Should I add shipping cost if the buyer pays for it?**
No. If shipping is paid by the buyer, you can leave this field blank. However, if
you offer free shipping, you must include it.

**Is depreciation (needles, hooks, etc.) included in the cost?**
Since it's usually a very small amount, it's typically not included. However, if
you use very expensive specialized equipment, you can add a small share to the
'accessories' section.
