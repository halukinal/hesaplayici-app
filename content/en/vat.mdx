# VAT Calculator — Turkey

VAT (Value Added Tax) — called KDV in Turkish — is the most common indirect tax in Turkey.
It applies to the sale of goods and services, imports, and similar transactions. The standard
VAT rate in Turkey is currently 20%. Additionally, reduced rates of 10% or 1% apply to
certain goods and services.

## How is VAT Calculated?

VAT calculation can be done in two directions:

**Adding VAT to net price (Excl. VAT → Incl. VAT):**
If you have a net (VAT-exclusive) amount and want the gross (VAT-inclusive) amount:

> Gross Amount = Net Amount × (1 + VAT Rate / 100)
> VAT Amount = Net Amount × (VAT Rate / 100)

Example: Adding 20% VAT to a net price of ₺1,000:
- VAT Amount = 1,000 × 0.20 = ₺200
- Gross Amount = 1,000 + 200 = **₺1,200**

**Removing VAT from gross price (Incl. VAT → Excl. VAT):**
If the invoice shows a VAT-inclusive price and you want the net amount:

> Net Amount = Gross Amount / (1 + VAT Rate / 100)
> VAT Amount = Gross Amount - Net Amount

Example: Removing 20% VAT from a gross price of ₺1,200:
- Net Amount = 1,200 / 1.20 = **₺1,000**
- VAT Amount = 1,200 - 1,000 = ₺200

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I remove VAT from a VAT-inclusive price?**
Divide the VAT-inclusive amount by (1 + VAT rate / 100). For example, for 20% VAT,
divide ₺1,200 by 1.20 to get a net amount of ₺1,000.

**What is the standard VAT rate in Turkey?**
As of 2024, the standard VAT rate in Turkey is 20%. Reduced rates of 10% and 1%
apply to selected goods and services.

**What is the difference between net and gross price?**
Net price is the amount excluding VAT. Gross price is the final amount the buyer pays,
including VAT.

**What is the VAT calculation formula?**
Add VAT: Gross = Net × (1 + rate/100). Remove VAT: Net = Gross / (1 + rate/100).

**Is the VAT rate the same for all products?**
No. In Turkey, rates of 1%, 10%, or 20% apply depending on the product or service category.
Check the official GİB list to determine which rate applies to a specific item.
