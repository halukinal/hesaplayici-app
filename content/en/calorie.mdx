# Calorie Calculator — Daily Energy Expenditure

Maintaining a healthy lifestyle, losing weight, or increasing muscle mass begins with
knowing how much energy your body needs daily. Every individual's metabolism varies
based on age, sex, weight, height, and daily activity level.

## What are Calorie Needs?

Your daily calorie requirement consists of two main components:

1.  **Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR):** The energy your body expends at rest to maintain
    vital functions (breathing, cell renewal, heartbeat) without any physical activity.
2.  **Physical Activity Energy:** The energy spent on all types of movement during the
    day, from walking to heavy sports training.

The sum of these two values is your **TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)**, which
represents your actual daily energy requirement.

## How is it Calculated?

Our calculator uses the **Mifflin-St Jeor** formula, widely considered to provide the
most accurate results in modern medical science. The formula works as follows:

**For Men (BMR):**
> (10 × weight [kg]) + (6.25 × height [cm]) - (5 × age [years]) + 5

**For Women (BMR):**
> (10 × weight [kg]) + (6.25 × height [cm]) - (5 × age [years]) - 161

The BMR value is then multiplied by an activity multiplier based on your lifestyle:
-   Sedentary (Office job): BMR × 1.2
-   Lightly Active (1-3 days/week): BMR × 1.375
-   Moderately Active (3-5 days/week): BMR × 1.55
-   Very Active (6-7 days/week): BMR × 1.725
-   Extra Active (Athlete / Physical job): BMR × 1.9

## What is Body Mass Index (BMI)?

In addition to calorie calculation, BMI is used to determine if your weight is in a
healthy range relative to your height. BMI is calculated with the following formula:

> BMI = Weight (kg) / (Height [m])²

BMI categories are generally defined as:
-   **Under 18.5:** Underweight
-   **18.5 - 24.9:** Normal Weight
-   **25 - 29.9:** Overweight
-   **30 and above:** Obese

## Tips for Weight Management

-   **To Lose Weight:** Consume approximately 300-500 calories less than your TDEE.
-   **To Gain Weight:** Consume 300-500 calories more than your TDEE.
-   **To Maintain:** Consume calories close to your TDEE value.

*Note: Losing 0.5 - 1 kg per week is generally more sustainable and healthier than
rapid weight loss.*

## Sources and Disclaimer

This calculator is based on the Mifflin-St Jeor (1990) equation. However, factors like
genetics, hormone levels, and muscle mass vary between individuals. It is recommended
to consult a dietitian or doctor for a personalized nutrition plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?**
BMR is the minimum amount of calories your body needs to maintain vital functions like
breathing, blood circulation, and cell production while at rest.

**How reliable is BMI (Body Mass Index)?**
BMI is a general health indicator but doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, or
fat distribution. It may be misleading for athletes.

**How many calories should I eat to lose weight?**
Generally, consuming 500 calories less than your daily energy expenditure (TDEE) leads
to about 0.5 kg of weight loss per week.

**How does activity level affect calorie needs?**
Calorie needs can vary by 1.5 to 2 times between a sedentary lifestyle and an extra
active one.

**Does drinking water help burn calories?**
While it doesn't burn calories directly, drinking water supports metabolism and can help
control calorie intake by promoting fullness.
