# What is Ideal Weight and How is it Calculated?

Ideal weight is much more than how you feel when you look in the mirror. Medically,
ideal weight is the body weight at which an individual is considered the healthiest
relative to their height, gender, and overall frame, carrying the lowest statistical
risk of chronic conditions like heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension.

## Is Ideal Weight a Single Number?

No. Body structures vary greatly from person to person. A fine-boned individual with
low muscle mass and a large-boned individual with high muscle mass will have different
healthy weights, even if they are the exact same height. Therefore, rather than giving
you a single absolute number, our calculator determines a "target" by averaging
various medical formulas and then presents a broad "healthy weight range".

## Ideal Weight Formulas Used in Medicine

Over the years, various formulas have been developed in the medical world to calculate
ideal weight. The most well-known are:

### 1. J.D. Robinson Formula (1983)
-   **Men:** 52 kg + 1.9 kg for each inch over 5 feet.
-   **Women:** 49 kg + 1.7 kg for each inch over 5 feet.

### 2. D.R. Miller Formula (1983)
-   **Men:** 56.2 kg + 1.41 kg for each inch over 5 feet.
-   **Women:** 53.1 kg + 1.36 kg for each inch over 5 feet.

### 3. G.J. Hamwi Formula (1964)
-   **Men:** 48.0 kg + 2.7 kg for each inch over 5 feet.
-   **Women:** 45.5 kg + 2.2 kg for each inch over 5 feet.

*Our calculator runs these universal formulas in the background, converting them to
the metric system (centimeters and kilograms) to provide you with the most reliable
average.*

## Healthy Weight Range (BMI Based)

According to standards set by the World Health Organization (WHO), individuals with
a Body Mass Index (BMI) between 18.5 and 24.9 are considered "healthy". The "Healthy
Weight Range" in our calculator is computed using this formula:

> **Lower Limit:** Height (in meters)² × 18.5
> **Upper Limit:** Height (in meters)² × 24.9

If your current weight is below this range, gaining weight is recommended for your
health; if it's above, losing weight is advised.

## For Whom Might it be Misleading?

Since standard formulas rely heavily on height and gender, they may not provide
accurate results in certain situations:
-   **Professional Athletes:** Due to high muscle mass, they may appear overweight or
    obese according to BMI and standard formulas.
-   **Pregnant Women:** Ideal weight calculation during pregnancy depends on entirely
    different dynamics and should be monitored by a doctor.
-   **Children and Teens:** Adult formulas are not used for growing individuals;
    instead, "percentile curves" are tracked.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does ideal weight change with age?**
Since height growth stops in adults (over 18), age does not have a large mathematical
effect on ideal weight. However, as we age, muscle mass tends to decrease and body fat
percentage may increase. Therefore, staying near the upper limit of the healthy range
is often considered normal for older adults.

**My calculated ideal weight seems too low/high, why?**
Standard formulas do not take muscle mass and bone density (large or small frame) into
account. If you exercise heavily, your muscle mass will weigh more, making these
results potentially unsuitable for you.

**Is Body Mass Index (BMI) the same as Ideal Weight?**
No. BMI is an index that tells you the ratio of your current weight to your height
and places you into categories (underweight, normal, overweight, obese). Ideal weight,
on the other hand, is a specific weight or weight range target you should aim for.

**How does bone structure affect ideal weight?**
The healthy weight for large-framed individuals can be up to 10% more than small-framed
individuals of the exact same height. This is why the healthy weight range is flexible.

**How can I reach my ideal weight?**
Through a healthy diet plan (caloric deficit or surplus) and regular exercise. You
can use our 'Calorie Calculator' to find your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)
and adjust your nutrition accordingly.
